GLOBAL CONSISTENCY ISSUES

1. mAP SUBSCRIPT FORMATTING
   At least four different LaTeX encodings are used for the mAP_{50-95}
   subscript across the thesis:
   (a) $\text{mAP}_{50-95}$ (hyphen in math mode -- renders as minus sign)
   (b) $\text{mAP}_{50\text{-}95}$ (hyphen in text mode within math)
   (c) $\mathrm{mAP}_{50\text{--}95}$ (en-dash in text mode within math)
   (d) mAP$_{50--95}$ (double hyphen in math mode -- renders as two minus signs)
   Recommendation: Standardise on one form globally. The most typographically
   correct form is (c) with \mathrm and an en-dash in text mode.

2. "SOCCER" vs "FOOTBALL" TERMINOLOGY
   The thesis title and many passages use "soccer", but numerous passages
   (especially in the literature review, methodology, and impact sections)
   use "football". These are used interchangeably without an explicit note
   establishing them as synonyms. Examples:
   - Title: "Soccer Analysis"
   - literature.tex caption: "Football Analytics Software Solutions"
   - methodology.tex: "football broadcast datasets", "football analytics"
   - impact.tex: "Football clubs, coaches"
   Recommendation: Add a footnote early (e.g. in the Introduction) stating
   that "soccer" and "football" are used interchangeably, or standardise
   on one term.

3. "FULL-STACK" / "FULL STACK" / "FULLSTACK" INCONSISTENCY
   Three different spellings are used:
   - "full-stack" (hyphenated, lower-case) -- most common
   - "Full-Stack" (hyphenated, capitalised) -- in some headings
   - "Fullstack" (one word) -- in Figure 17 caption (results.tex line 266)
   - "Full Stack" (two words, no hyphen) -- occasionally
   Recommendation: Standardise on "full-stack" (hyphenated) as an adjective
   and "full stack" (two words) as a noun.

4. FIGURE CAPTION PLACEMENT INCONSISTENCY
   In the literature review (e.g. Figure 4, line 218-222 of literature.tex),
   \caption is placed after \includegraphics (caption below figure). In the
   results section (e.g. Figures 5-17), \caption is placed before
   \includegraphics (caption above figure). Academic convention varies, but
   placement should be consistent throughout.
   Recommendation: Choose one placement (above or below) and apply
   consistently to all figures.

5. DEGREE SYMBOL FORMATTING
   Temperatures are expressed using at least three formats:
   - "$85^\circ$" (without C) -- introduction.tex
   - "85$^\circ$C" -- methodology.tex
   - "68\textdegree C" -- discussion.tex
   - "68°C" -- abstract.tex
   Recommendation: Standardise on "$85\,^\circ\mathrm{C}$" or use the
   siunitx package for consistent unit formatting.

6. REFERENCE FORMATTING
   Cross-references to figures and tables are generally consistent ("Figure",
   "Table", "Section"), but the compression summary tables use the
   \begin{figure} environment despite containing tabular data alongside
   plots. This is acceptable but could confuse automated list generation.

7. TENSE CONSISTENCY
   The thesis predominantly uses passive voice and present tense for
   describing results ("Figure X shows...", "As can be seen..."), but
   occasionally shifts to active voice or past tense within the same
   section. This is minor but worth a consistency pass.

8. "KEY-POINT" vs "KEYPOINT"
   Most of the thesis uses "keypoint" (one word), but the section heading
   in literature.tex uses "Key-point Detection" (hyphenated, line 163).
   Recommendation: Use "keypoint" consistently.


TECHNICAL / FACTUAL / LOGICAL CONCERNS TO VERIFY

1. CITATION ERROR: FASTER R-CNN CITED AS RT-DETR
   (results.tex, line 29)
   "Faster R-CNN \cite{Zhao2023RTDETR}" -- Zhao2023RTDETR is the RT-DETR
   paper, not Faster R-CNN. This is almost certainly a citation key mix-up.
   The correct key for Faster R-CNN is 7485869.

2. CITATION ERROR: TENNIS PAPER CITED FOR SOCCER PITCH KEYPOINTS
   (literature.tex, line 10)
   \cite{10846339} points to a paper on tennis analysis, but the surrounding
   text discusses soccer pitch markings. The intended reference may be
   IEEE2024PitchKeypoints (defined in references.bib but never cited).

3. GPU ARCHITECTURE CONTRADICTION
   (literature.tex, lines 247 vs 259)
   The Jetson Orin Nano is correctly identified as "Ampere 1024-core" in
   Table 5 (line 247), but incorrectly described as having a "1024-core
   Maxwell GPU" in the Current Limitations subsection (line 259).

4. FLOPS ACRONYM DEFINITION
   (literature.tex, line 7)
   "515B Floating Point Operations Per Inference (FLOPS)" -- FLOPS
   conventionally means "per Second". The text appears to mean total
   operations per inference, for which the correct abbreviation would be
   FLOPs (lower-case s). This should be verified and corrected.

5. SIGN/DESCRIPTION MISMATCH IN POSE COMBO RESULTS
   (results.tex, line 71)
   "Distilled+Accelerated producing a -0.9% mAP improvement" -- a negative
   change described as an "improvement" is contradictory. Verify whether
   the change was positive (an actual improvement) or negative (a cost).

6. POWER LIMITS UNDER MAXN MODE
   (methodology.tex, lines 77-87 vs introduction.tex, lines 22-24)
   The introduction establishes limits based on 80% of 25W = 20W. However,
   the methodology notes that MAXN mode "does not have a fixed power limit".
   The 25W figure comes from the 25W power mode, not MAXN. Verify whether
   the 25W baseline is still appropriate for MAXN mode. The Jetson Orin
   Nano Super Developer Kit has different power profiles from the standard
   module; ensure the quoted specifications match the exact hardware used.

7. mAP BASELINE DISCREPANCY
   (literature.tex line 68 vs results.tex line 164)
   The literature review states that YOLO models can produce "mAP_{50-95}
   of up to 92%" for soccer object detection. The results section then
   uses "0.915" as the strong baseline for the 15% threshold calculation.
   While these could refer to different configurations, the discrepancy
   (92% vs 91.5%) should be verified or explained.

8. BALL DETECTION RECALL IMPROVEMENT CLAIM
   (literature.tex, line 326)
   "increasing resolution from 640px to 1200px has been shown to improve
   ball detection recall by up to 96%" -- this is ambiguous. Does recall
   improve by 96 percentage points, improve to 96%, or improve by a factor
   of 96%? The original source should be checked and the phrasing clarified.

9. PRUNING TERMINOLOGY IN FULL-STACK TABLE
   (images/fullstack.tex, line 49)
   "80% denotes 80% structured pruning" is ambiguous. In the methodology,
   pruning is defined by retention level (80% retention = 20% pruned).
   The table note could be read as 80% of weights removed. This should be
   clarified to match the methodology's terminology.

10. YOLO26 TECHNICAL CLAIMS
    (literature.tex, line 229; discussion.tex, lines 33-35)
    Several specific architectural claims about YOLO26 (attention mechanism,
    Progressive Loss Balancing, MuSGD, small-target-aware labelling) are
    cited to \cite{UltralyticsYOLO}, which is the general Ultralytics
    documentation. Since YOLO26 is very recent, verify that these
    architectural details are accurately described and properly sourced.

11. KNOWLEDGE DISTILLATION LATENCY ARTEFACT
    (results.tex, lines 48-49; results.tex, line 65)
    The text attributes negative latency changes for distillation to an
    "inefficient use of PyTorch to implement knowledge distillation" and
    says this "does not materially affect the quality of analysis". This
    claim should be verified -- if the PyTorch implementation was genuinely
    inefficient, this may also have affected the distillation training
    quality, not just inference speed.

12. SINGLE-VIDEO EVALUATION LIMITATION
    (results.tex, line 236)
    The full-stack evaluation uses a single 10-minute video. While this is
    acknowledged as a limitation, the claims about pass mAP approaching
    the SoccerNet baseline should be qualified more strongly given the
    single-sample evaluation. Statistical confidence in the reported
    metrics cannot be established from one video.
